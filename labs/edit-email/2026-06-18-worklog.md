# like-human-do — experiment worklog

## What this lab is for

Finding, by experiment, a standing `edit-email` capability that makes a follow-up read as a person wrote it to someone they like, not as a project with an ask dressed in warmth. The deliverable shipped in `skills/edit-email/like-human-do.md`; rule provenance in `skills/edit-email/like-human-do.nswp.md`. This lab is kept in-tree so the whole search can be re-run and compared when a new model arrives.

## Anonymization

All names here are stable placeholders, not real people. A local decode key is at `key.tsv` (gitignored). The two recipients: **Dan Marsh** (a distiller, runs Ridgeline Distillery up the mountain; told us at the event about a white-whale trademark, "Snowcap", that he held and lost) and **Nadia Brook** (organised the trade exchange; her job is getting operators listed and sold through hotel desks). The sender is **Sam** of **Hillside Farm**; **Mia** is Sam's marketing manager. If you are reading this and "Dan" means nothing to you, that is the anonymization working; consult `key.tsv`.

## Methodology

**The harness: fork, compose, score.**

- *Fork, not re-prompt.* Each test forks the real source conversation (`claude -p --resume <id> --fork-session`), so the authoring AI carries the exact prior context of the meeting (the recordings, the email threads, the debrief) into composing the email. This is the only way the test reflects the real task; a clean prompt would strip the context the email is supposed to draw on. The author is therefore blind to nothing about the meeting and blind to one thing on purpose: the user's hand-written reference (written later, in a different session), which the fork cannot see.
- *Variant isolation.* Each condition is a minimal plugin copy under `variants/<id>/`, loaded with `--plugin-dir`, so the real skill stays untouched and conditions cannot collide. Every variant emits a `[[id]]` marker as its first line; if the marker is absent from the output, the fork loaded some other plugin and the run is void. This caught nothing in the end but is the guard that makes "my edit was tested" a fact, not a hope.
- *Capture.* `--output-format json`; the `result` field is the produced email, plus cost and the fork's own session id, all logged to `runs/<name>.json` and indexed in `manifest.tsv` (`sqlite3 .mode tabs`).

**The gate: a blind recipient-simulation.** A context-free agent role-plays the recipient, is never told the email is AI-written, never shown the rubric, never shown another version, and is asked only: would you reply, and does anything feel off / written to get something / mass-sent. The decision is whether it senses a pitch. This is the spine because an AI judge that knows it is grading AI shares the author's blind spot; a recipient just reacting does not. Rubric in `rubric.md`, prompt and personas in `recipient-sim.md`.

**A methodology bug found and fixed mid-run.** The first recipient personas had no memory of their own lives, so when an email referenced a genuine shared detail (Dan's Snowcap trademark, which Dan himself had raised), the memory-less "Dan" read it as "you've got me mixed up" and false-flagged a real connection as a mistake. Fix: the persona must carry the recipient's real memories. The informed persona is the valid instrument; the naive blind one penalises exactly the move we are trying to reward. All gating verdicts that matter were re-run informed.

**What blindness held where (important for reading the outcome):**
- The *authors* (forks) never saw either human reference. Every AI email was composed without sight of the gold.
- The *judges* (recipient-sims) saw the human Dan reference exactly once, in Round 1, blind among four AI drafts, as a calibration anchor; it was the only one that passed. The human Nadia reference was never shown to any judge.

## The matrix, and how thinly each cell was tested

This was an **adaptive sequential search, not a factorial**. Each round changed one thing in response to the last round's observed failure, so most cells are n=1. Being honest about that is the point of this section.

|---|---|---|---|---|

| Pass count | 0-pass compose-time (all A/B variants); subeditor-as-backstop (baseline + 2 final) | 16 / 3 | — | 0-pass gated; 1-pass-only and impersonator passes **not isolated at all** |

Gating coverage across the 18 forks: **4 blind-sim PASS, 5 blind-sim FAIL, 1 inferred (B4-Dan rep2, from rep1), 3 judged by me without a sim (the cold case and the 2 final end-to-end runs), 5 never gated** (B1-Nadia, B1-Sonnet, B3-Dan, B3-Nadia, B4-Sonnet — read by inspection only). So roughly half the runs carry a real blind verdict. That is enough to locate the levers but not to put confidence intervals on anything.

## Assumptions that turned out untrue

1. **"Tell the model the diagnosis and it will fix it."** Mostly false. Naming the failure ("use a real shared detail", "be warmer") was largely inert; the model needed the move forced as a procedure (enumerate non-business things first), not described.
2. **"A great-writer exemplar will carry the voice."** False. "Write as Charles Lamb would" (A2) did not even fix the surface, let alone the structure; it kept the business-led opening, the title block, the hedged referral. Exemplar framing was dropped.
3. **"Minimal trust works — just say write what you'd send."** False. A3 ≈ baseline. The model's default register *is* the AI register; absent specific pressure it returns to it.
4. **"Give-don't-seek is the rule."** False as an absolute. Taken literally (B1) it deleted Nadia's legitimate brochure ask, the actual purpose of that email. The true rule is to *classify* the ask: cut a grab, keep a genuine humble ask.
5. **"Forcing the model to use a shared detail is safe."** False where no genuine detail exists. On Nadia, B1 bent an unrelated log fact (two club members at an after-hours session) into a fake closeness. Forcing retrieval has a confabulation/misattribution failure mode; the rule had to add "never force one".
6. **"More caution about the connection point is safer."** False. B3's stronger caution made the model abandon the genuine detail (Snowcap) entirely and revert to business. Over-correction was worse than the fault it fixed.
7. **"The blind recipient-sim is valid as-is."** False until the persona carried the recipient's real memory (see methodology bug).
8. **"Closeness to the user's email is the target."** Set aside by design, and the result vindicates it: the best Dan email shares almost no content with the user's (it found its own genuine detail), yet passes the same gate.

## How the result was reached (round by round)

- **R0 — mechanics + instruments.** Confirmed the fork/`--plugin-dir`/`--model`/json stack composes and that `/edit-email` triggers; built the rubric and recipient-sim; captured the two references. Baseline (current skill, no LHD) is the control: AI register intact.
- **R1 — altitude.** A1/A2/A3 on Dan, one rep each, all gated. Ordering: prescriptive > exemplar > minimal. But the decisive event was the gate: baseline and all three altitudes FAILED, and the human reference was the only PASS, every AI version read as "the referral swap is the real engine, dressed as a hello." That located the real problem below altitude: the instrumental ask.
- **R2 — give-don't-grab + forced retrieval (B1).** On Dan: cut the referral, forced a non-business enumeration; surfaced Snowcap and PASSED. On Nadia: over-corrected, deleted the legitimate ask and bent a detail into false closeness (FAIL by inspection).
- **R3 — classify the ask (B2).** Nadia PASSED cleanly (humble ask kept, Mia introduced, reply "today"). But B2 re-narrated Snowcap and added a verdict on Dan's affairs; the informed sim read it as flattery/softening and FAILED it.
- **R4 — light-touch caution (B3).** Over-shot: the model dropped Snowcap entirely and reverted to business; Nadia mis-credited the pricing steer. Worse.
- **R5 — synthesis (B4) + validation.** B2's ask-classification + B1's lighter connection wording + one sentence against verdicts. Dan PASSED on both reps, Nadia PASSED, cold first-contact handled honestly (no manufactured intimacy). Final end-to-end on the *committed* skill (compose step + subeditor backstop) passed for both; the subeditor earned its place by cutting a residual my-takeaway clause on Dan.

## Opus vs Sonnet — what was and was not measured

Honest status: **not a scored comparison.** Sonnet was run twice (B1-Dan, B4-Dan) and assessed by inspection only; neither went through the blind gate. On identical B4 guidance, Sonnet leaked its silent working into the output, used an em dash against the explicit rule, used a ranking word ("stuck"), and led with the business takeaway; Opus obeyed all of these. That is enough to prefer Opus for rule-compliance, but it is an n=1, ungated read, not a pass-rate difference. A real model comparison (below) is the first thing a redo should fix.

Your reference: *"Hi Dan, It was a pleasure to meet you at the tourism exchange, and thanks for editing my short message to the hoteliers. Would you come over for our open day on the 20th? Otherwise, come and have a coffee at our place. I'll be in Australia for two to three months. I also mean to visit the distillery, with my parents. **Resemblance: tone and shape high, content low.** Both are short, first-name, give-weighted, no pitch. None of that is in the fork's context. The AI substituted the one genuine in-context detail it did have (Snowcap) and reached the same human register by a different road. This is the clearest evidence that resemblance is not the success metric: the best AI Dan email is a good human email that is not your email.

**Resemblance: high, in content too.** Here the substance lives in the fork's context (the brochure, the whole-day insight, Mia), so the AI converged on nearly your email: same thank-you, same pivot to the flyer, same humble ask honouring her expertise, same Mia cc. When the material is available, the skill reaches your version closely; when it is not (Dan), it reaches your register but not your content.

## If I started this today

The path was adaptive and wasteful at the edges. A cleaner run:

1. **Skip the altitude round.** Prescriptive obviously wins; do not spend three gated runs proving exemplar and minimal are weak. Start at the levers.
2. **Lead with the two that mattered** (give-don't-grab, classify-the-ask) and treat the connection-point handling as the fine-tuning, since that is where the back-and-forth (B2 heavy → B3 abandon → B4 light) actually happened.
3. **Gate every cell, including Sonnet, with n≥3 reps**, and report a pass-rate per cell with the spread, instead of n=1 verdicts. This is the biggest validity gap: Opus-vs-Sonnet is currently an opinion, not a measurement.
4. **Informed personas from the first sim**, not after the false-flag.
5. **Hold the human reference as a scored comparator from the start**, judged blind among the AI versions every round, so "at or above the reference" is a number, not my call.
6. **Add a second, differently-built judge** (different model or a from-the-cold-reader frame) to reduce shared blind spot, and only trust a PASS both judges agree on.
7. **More recipients than two**, including two or three cold cases, so the standing feature is not tuned to a single warm-peer and a single humble-ask shape.
8. **Separate the retrieval step as its own measured stage** (did it surface the genuine detail? did it confabulate?), since that is where the subtle failures (misattribution, abandonment) live.

## Stats

- **18 forks** of the source conversation; **11 blind recipient-simulations** (context-free, not forks). 6 rounds (R0 mechanics through R5, plus final end-to-end).
- Gating: 4 blind-sim PASS, 5 FAIL, 1 inferred, 3 judged-by-lead, 5 ungated. Both target recipients PASS at B4 (Dan across 2 reps, Nadia once), plus the cold case by judgment.
- Models: Opus 14 runs (gated), Sonnet 2 runs (ungated, inspection only).
- Cost ≈ US$23 (the source conversation caches after the first fork; batched forks ran ~US$0.5, cold-cache ones ~US$2.5–3).
- Winner: variant B4, Opus, compose-time, subeditor as backstop. Levers in order of force: give-don't-grab; keep a genuine humble ask but strip positioning; a real connection point touched lightly, never re-narrated, never forced; surface levers (no ranking, brevity, person register, plain words) — necessary, not sufficient.
