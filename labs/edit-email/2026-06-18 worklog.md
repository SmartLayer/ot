# like-human-do — experiment worklog

## What this lab is for

Finding, by experiment, a standing `edit-email` capability that makes a follow-up read as a person wrote it to someone they like, not as a project with an ask dressed in warmth. The deliverable shipped in `skills/edit-email/like-human-do.md` (committed `579ea73`); rule provenance is in `skills/edit-email/like-human-do.nswp.md`. This worklog traces how we got there and is kept so the whole thing can be re-run and compared when a new model arrives.

## How to reproduce

Each experimental condition is a minimal plugin copy under `variants/<id>/`, carrying a `[[id]]` marker that proves the edited plugin loaded (not an installed shadow). A run forks the source conversation and asks it to write the email through the skill:

```
claude -p --resume <SOURCE_SESSION> --fork-session -n "<run-name>" \
  --plugin-dir labs/edit-email/variants/<id> \
  --model opus --allowed-tools "Skill Read" --output-format json \
  "SCOPE: text only, no files/git/mail, ignore pending tasks. Using /edit-email, write the follow-up from me to <recipient>. Follow the skill exactly."
```

- **Source conversation:** session `67168ade-1284-414b-86e3-6b1522841522` ("write-email-lhd"), a real meeting debrief. It lives in `~/.claude/projects/-home-user-code-ot-skills-edit-email/` on the user's machine, NOT in this repo (size and third-party PII). Reproduction needs that session present; without it, fork the equivalent context.
- **To test a new model:** swap `--model opus` for `--model fable` (and `Skill Task Read` if the variant spawns the subeditor). Compare outputs against `ground-truth/*.txt` (the user's hand-written references) and against this run's outputs in `runs/*.json`.
- **Scoring** is a blind recipient-simulation: a context-free agent role-playing the recipient, never told the email is AI-written, asked only "would you reply, and does anything feel off / written to get something / mass-sent." The gate is whether it senses a pitch. Prompt and personas in `recipient-sim.md`; rubric in `rubric.md`. The persona must carry the recipient's real memories, or it false-flags a genuine shared detail as wrong.

`manifest.tsv` is the index: every run name maps to its fork session id, config, and verdict. Edit it with `sqlite3` `.mode tabs`.

## Trace, round by round

- **R0 — mechanics + instruments.** Confirmed `--plugin-dir` + `--resume` + `--fork-session` + `--model` + `--output-format json` compose, that a fork loads the edited skill, and that `/edit-email` triggers. Built `rubric.md`, `recipient-sim.md`, captured the two reference emails. Baseline (current skill, no LHD) left the email in its AI register: the control.
- **R1 — altitude.** Three variants at equal everything-else on Dan. A1 (prescriptive named moves), A2 (evocative exemplar, "write as Charles Lamb would"), A3 (minimal, "write what you'd send"). Result: prescriptive > exemplar > minimal. A1 fixed the surface (length, flattery, register); A2 was warmer prose but kept the business lead, title block, hedged referral; A3 ≈ baseline. Blind recipient-sims: all four (incl. baseline) FAILED the gate; only the human reference passed. The reader's verdict on every AI version: "the referral swap is the real engine, dressed as a hello."
- **R2 — the decisive lever.** B1 added give-don't-seek (cut the instrumental ask) plus a forced pre-draft enumeration of non-business things the recipient shared. On Dan it surfaced Snowcap (the real shared moment every prior variant skipped) and PASSED the gate. But on Nadia it over-corrected: deleted her legitimate brochure-review ask (the email's whole point) and bent an unrelated detail (Alex and Theo's "the Friday club") into a false closeness. Sonnet on B1 leaked its working and used an em-dash.
- **R3 — classify the ask.** B2 split the want into a grab (cut) versus a genuine humble ask (keep, bare, no positioning). Nadia PASSED cleanly (humble ask kept, Mia introduced, reply "today"). But B2's heavier Snowcap handling on Dan re-narrated the story and appended a verdict ("should have stayed yours"), which the informed sim read as flattery and softening: FAIL.
- **R4 — over-correction.** B3 added a caution against re-narration; the model overshot and abandoned Snowcap entirely, reverting to the business opening. Nadia credited the wrong person with the pricing steer. Worse, not better.
- **R5 — synthesis and validation.** B4 = B2's ask-classification + B1's lighter connection-point wording + one sentence against verdicts. Stable across two Dan reps (light Snowcap touch, give, no grab) and Nadia (humble ask kept). Informed sims: both PASS, both would reply. Cold first-contact (hotel, no history): handled honestly, no manufactured intimacy, give-weighted ask. Sonnet on B4 still used an em-dash, a ranking word, and led with business: Opus preferred. Final end-to-end run on the committed skill (compose step + cold-reader subeditor backstop) passed for both; the subeditor caught one residual "my-takeaway" clause on Dan.

## Outcome

**Winner: variant B4, Opus, compose-time (0-pass), subeditor as backstop.** The corrective levers, in order of force: (1) give don't grab; (2) keep a genuine humble ask but strip positioning; (3) a real connection point touched lightly, never re-narrated, never forced; (4) surface levers (no ranking, brevity, person register, plain words) — necessary, not sufficient. Inert or harmful: exemplar framing, minimal framing, heavy re-narration, absolute give-don't-seek, over-caution.

The pre-skill versions failed a blind recipient as "written to get something"; the B4 versions passed as "feels written to me, no pitch," would-reply. B4 found its own connection point (Snowcap) rather than copying the user's reference, which leans on facts the AI cannot see (the gin, his parents, the dated event, the hotelier-message edit). Resemblance was not the target and was not required.
